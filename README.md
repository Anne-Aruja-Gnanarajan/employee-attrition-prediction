Pharma Co, a pharmaceuticals company, has expressed concerns regarding employee dissatisfaction and staff turnover. Employee attrition can result in significant replacement costs, loss of company knowledge and customer service interruptions (Bhuvaneswari & Kavitha, 2022). In order to address the problem of attrition, Pharma Co has provided a 1000-observation employee dataset, with the binary variable for attrition included. 

The purpose of this report is to (1) identify and analyse two predictive models that were built to predict whether an employee will leave Pharma Co, and (2) use the predictors from these models to draw further insights and provide recommendations to address the problem of employee attrition. This report also explains the process by which the two predictive models were selected, and critically discusses the performance of the two predictive models. 

As a brief overview, the two models were selected using the process of “forward stepwise selection” (discussed in the “Analysis Methodology” section) with models being evaluated using cross-validated “area under the curve” (AUC) (Appendix B and Appendix C). Final models were evaluated using AUC, accuracy, specificity and sensitivity (outlined in the “Analysis Methodology”, Appendix B and Appendix C). 

The two models included in this report are a (1) logistic regression model and (2) decision tree model.
